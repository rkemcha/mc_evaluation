## 8. Admission Requirements:
*   A Bachelor's degree (or equivalent professional experience) in Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, or a closely related technical field.
*   Demonstrated proficiency in at least one modern programming language (e.g., Python, Java, C++).
*   Fundamental understanding of data structures, algorithms, and basic networking concepts (e.g., TCP/IP).
*   Familiarity with database systems (SQL/NoSQL) and cloud computing platforms (e.g., AWS, Azure, Google Cloud) is highly recommended.

## 9. Learning Objectives:
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, participants will be able to:
*   **Analyze** the core technological components (e.g., sensors, networks, cloud platforms) and architectural frameworks of smart city ecosystems.
*   **Design and propose** innovative, data-driven solutions for complex urban challenges (e.g., traffic management, energy efficiency) using IoT, AI, and advanced data analytics techniques.
*   **Implement and evaluate** prototype smart city applications through hands-on lab exercises and a significant capstone project, demonstrating proficiency in relevant programming languages and platforms.
*   **Assess** the ethical, privacy, **security,** and societal implications of deploying smart city technologies and **design resilient and responsible deployment strategies.**
*   **Evaluate and apply** appropriate cybersecurity measures and data protection frameworks for smart city IoT devices, networks, and platforms.
*   **Critically appraise** emerging research trends, **including advanced AI paradigms like Generative AI,** and future directions in urban computing and smart city development, contributing to ongoing innovation.

## 10. Content Description:
*   **Module 1: Smart City Foundations and IoT Infrastructure**
    *   Introduction to Smart Cities: Concepts, Drivers, and Challenges
    *   IoT Architectures and Communication Protocols (5G, LPWAN, etc.)
    *   Sensor Networks and Data Acquisition
    *   Edge Computing for Real-time Processing
*   **Module 2: Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence for Urban Environments**
    *   Big Data Management and Storage
    *   Data Mining and Machine Learning Techniques for Urban Data
    *   AI-powered Decision Support Systems
    *   Predictive Analytics for Urban Planning
*   **Module 3: Smart City Applications and Services**
    *   Intelligent Transportation Systems
    *   Smart Energy Grids and Resource Management
    *   Smart Buildings and Infrastructure
    *   Public Safety and Emergency Response Systems
*   **Module 4: Smart City Cybersecurity and Data Governance**
    *   IoT device security (hardware, firmware, authentication)
    *   Network security in urban environments (5G, LPWAN, mesh networks)
    *   Data privacy by design and regulatory compliance (GDPR, CCPA in urban context)
    *   Threat modeling and risk assessment for smart city infrastructure
    *   Secure software development practices for urban applications
    *   Introduction to blockchain for secure urban data (brief overview)

## 15. Learning Activities:
*   Weekly Hands-on Lab Assignments: Practical exercises focusing on programming IoT devices, collecting and processing sensor data, integrating with smart city platforms, and implementing secure communication/data handling.
*   Case Study Analysis: Participants will analyze real-world smart city deployments, critically evaluating their technical design, societal impact, ethical considerations, and potential applications/risks of emerging technologies like Generative AI.
*   Capstone Project: A comprehensive project where participants design, develop, and present a prototype smart city solution (e.g., smart waste management, intelligent traffic system) addressing a specific urban problem. Includes a written report and presentation. Projects must now explicitly include a "Security and Privacy Impact Assessment" section, detailing potential vulnerabilities, proposed mitigation strategies, and compliance with data privacy regulations. Projects involving data collection must demonstrate secure data handling. An optional advanced topic for projects could involve exploring Generative AI applications.
*   Peer Review and Participation: Active engagement in online discussions, code reviews of peers' work, and constructive feedback.
