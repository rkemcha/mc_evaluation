## 9. Learning Objectives (Learning Outcomes)
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, participants will be able to:
*   **LO1:** Analyze diverse educational systems and qualification frameworks globally.
*   **LO2:** Apply established methodologies and standards for evaluating foreign academic and professional credentials.
*   **LO3:** Critically assess the authenticity and comparability of educational documents.
*   **LO4:** Identify and interpret relevant legal and ethical considerations in credential evaluation.
*   **LO5:** Communicate credential evaluation findings clearly and professionally in written reports.

## 10. Content Description (Module Breakdown)

**Total Workload Target: 81 hours (3 ECTS)**

### Module 1: Foundations of Credential Evaluation
*   **Learning Outcomes Addressed:** LO1, LO4
*   **Content:**
    *   Introduction to credential evaluation: purpose, scope, and importance.
    *   Key terminology and concepts (e.g., credential, qualification framework, accreditation).
    *   Overview of major global educational systems (e.g., Bologna Process, US system, UK system, Chinese system).
    *   Introduction to national and international qualification frameworks (e.g., EQF, NQF).
    *   Ethical considerations and legal frameworks in credential evaluation.

### Module 2: Methodologies and Standards
*   **Learning Outcomes Addressed:** LO2, LO3
*   **Content:**
    *   Introduction to common evaluation methodologies (e.g., document-by-document, course-by-course, credential equivalency).
    *   Key resources and databases for credential evaluation (e.g., NACES members, ENIC-NARIC centres, World Education Services).
    *   Understanding and interpreting official documents (transcripts, diplomas, certificates, mark sheets).
    *   Identifying red flags and indicators of fraudulent documents.
    *   Accreditation bodies and their significance.

### Module 3: Practical Application and Reporting
*   **Learning Outcomes Addressed:** LO2, LO3, LO5
*   **Content:**
    *   Developing a comprehensive credential evaluation report.
    *   Formulating equivalency statements and recommendations.
    *   Best practices for clear, concise, and defensible reporting.
    *   Responding to inquiries and defending evaluation decisions.
    *   Review of industry standards and best practices for reporting.

## 15. Learning Activities
*   **Interactive Readings:** Short articles and case studies with embedded questions.
*   **"System Explorer" Challenge (Gamified):** Learners research and present a brief overview of an unfamiliar educational system.
*   **Discussion Forum:** Debate on ethical dilemmas.
*   **Document Analysis Workshop:** Guided practice in analyzing various international academic documents.
*   **"Fraud Fighter" Game (Gamified):** Learners identify discrepancies in simulated documents.
*   **Case Study Analysis & Report:** Participants evaluate simulated credential portfolios and draft reports.
*   **Peer Review Exercise (Gamified):** Learners review and provide feedback on peer reports using rubrics.
*   **"Decision Defender" Scenario (Gamified):** Learners justify evaluation decisions against objections.
*   **Final Credential Evaluation Project:** Comprehensive evaluation of a complex credential portfolio.

## 18. Assessment Methods

*   **Case Study Analysis & Report:** Participants evaluate a simulated credential portfolio from a hypothetical applicant and prepare a professional evaluation report.
*   **Peer Review Exercise:** Participants review and provide feedback on a peer's case study analysis, using a provided rubric.
*   **Final Credential Evaluation Project:** A comprehensive evaluation of a complex, multi-document credential portfolio, culminating in a detailed written report and justification of findings.
