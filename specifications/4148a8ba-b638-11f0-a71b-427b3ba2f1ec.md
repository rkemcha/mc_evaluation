## 9. Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
*   Critically analyze the landscape of microcredentials, including their types, purpose, and impact on education and employment.
*   Apply established principles and frameworks for credential evaluation to the specific context of microcredentials.
*   Design and implement robust evaluation methodologies for assessing the quality, validity, and recognition of microcredentials.
*   Evaluate the alignment of microcredentials with industry standards, labor market needs, and existing qualification frameworks.
*   Identify and address ethical, legal, and policy considerations in the evaluation and recognition of microcredentials.
*   Develop recommendations for best practices in the design, issuance, and evaluation of microcredentials within an institutional or organizational context.

## 10. Content Description

**Total Workload:** 15 ECTS = 420 hours (assuming 1 ECTS = 28 hours)

### Module 1: Foundations of Microcredentials
*   **Workload:** 80 hours (~2.85 ECTS)
*   **Description:** This module introduces the concept, types, benefits, and challenges of microcredentials. Learners will analyze the global landscape, drivers for their emergence, and their evolving role in education and employment.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Understand the concept, types, benefits, and challenges of microcredentials; analyze the global landscape and drivers for their emergence.

### Module 2: Principles of Credential Evaluation
*   **Workload:** 85 hours (~3.0 ECTS)
*   **Description:** This module covers established principles and methodologies for evaluating educational credentials. Learners will compare different credentialing frameworks (e.g., EQF, national qualification frameworks) and their relevance.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Identify key principles and methodologies for evaluating educational credentials; compare different credentialing frameworks and their relevance.

### Module 3: Specifics of Microcredential Evaluation
*   **Workload:** 90 hours (~3.2 ECTS)
*   **Description:** This module focuses on adapting general evaluation principles to the unique characteristics of microcredentials. It includes assessing the quality and validity of digital credentials and badging systems.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Adapt general evaluation principles to the unique characteristics of microcredentials; assess the quality and validity of digital credentials and badging systems.

### Module 4: Quality Assurance and Best Practices
*   **Workload:** 85 hours (~3.0 ECTS)
*   **Description:** Learners will explore quality assurance mechanisms for microcredentials, analyze ethical, legal, and policy considerations, and identify future trends and policy implications in the field.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Understand quality assurance mechanisms for microcredentials; analyze ethical and legal considerations; identify future trends and policy implications.

### Module 5: Practical Application and Project
*   **Workload:** 80 hours (~2.85 ECTS)
*   **Description:** This capstone module requires learners to synthesize and apply all acquired knowledge to conduct a full evaluation of a real or simulated microcredential, culminating in a comprehensive report and presentation.
*   **Learning Outcomes:** Synthesize and apply knowledge to conduct a full evaluation of a microcredential; present findings and recommendations effectively.
