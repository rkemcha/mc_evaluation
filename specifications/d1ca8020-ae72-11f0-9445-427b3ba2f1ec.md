### **Section 3: Learning**

#### 12. Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, the learner will be able to:
1.  **LO1**: Define and apply the fundamental principles of project management, including scope, time, cost, and quality management.
2.  **LO2**: Develop a comprehensive project plan for a small-scale project, including a work breakdown structure, schedule, and budget.
3.  **LO3**: Compare and contrast Waterfall and Agile project management methodologies and select the appropriate approach for different project scenarios.
4.  **LO4**: Identify and analyze project stakeholders and develop strategies for effective communication and engagement.
5.  **LO5**: Apply basic risk management techniques to identify, assess, and mitigate common project risks.
6.  **LO6**: Demonstrate the use of common project management software tools for planning and tracking.

#### 13. Teaching and Learning Methods
- **Active Learning**: Learners engage with the material through case study analysis, interactive discussions, and problem-solving activities.
- **Project-Based Learning**: The core of the course is a cumulative project where learners apply concepts to a real-world scenario, from initiation to closure.
- **Inquiry-Based Learning**: Learners are encouraged to investigate project management challenges, research solutions, and reflect on their decision-making processes during simulations.

#### 14. Schedule/Syllabus
- **Week 1**: Introduction to Project Management & The Project Lifecycle
- **Week 2**: Project Initiation & Scope Management
- **Week 3**: Project Planning: Time & Cost Management
- **Week 4**: Project Execution: Quality & Stakeholder Management
- **Week 5**: Introduction to Agile & Scrum
- **Week 6**: Agile Simulation & Project Portfolio Submission

#### 15. Entry Requirements/Prerequisites
- There are no formal prerequisites for this microcredential. It is open to learners from all backgrounds.

#### 16. Recommended Prior Knowledge
- A basic understanding of general business concepts and proficiency with standard office software are recommended but not required.

### **Section 4: Assessment**

#### 17. Assessment Rationale
- The assessment strategy is designed to be authentic and comprehensive, ensuring learners can both understand and apply project management concepts. Assessments are directly mapped to the learning outcomes and increase in complexity, culminating in a portfolio that demonstrates integrated skills.

#### 18. Assessment Methods
- **Knowledge Quizzes (20%)**: A series of online quizzes to assess understanding of core terminology and concepts (Assesses LO1).
- **Agile Simulation (40%)**: A team-based simulation where learners manage a project using an Agile framework. This assessment specifically evaluates the learner's **autonomy** in making iterative decisions under changing conditions and their **responsibility** for sprint outcomes and team collaboration (Assesses LO3, LO4).
- **Final Project Portfolio (40%)**: A comprehensive submission that includes a complete project plan, risk register, and stakeholder communication plan for a given scenario. The portfolio requires a **reflective analysis on managing unpredictable project scenarios**, demonstrating the learner's ability to take **responsibility** for the project's strategic direction and exercise **autonomy** in problem-solving (Assesses LO2, LO5, LO6).

#### 19. Grading Scheme
- **Passing Grade**: A minimum overall score of 60% is required to pass.
- **Scheme**: Graded on a standard A-F scale.
