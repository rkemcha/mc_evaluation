### **9. Learning Objectives**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
1.  Describe and compare traditional (Waterfall) and modern (Agile) project management lifecycles.
2.  Apply core Agile and Scrum concepts, such as sprints, user stories, and backlogs, to a project scenario.
3.  Develop a basic project charter and scope statement for a given scenario.
4.  Create a simple work breakdown structure (WBS) and project schedule.
5.  Apply basic techniques for identifying and managing project risks and stakeholders.
6.  Collaborate effectively within a team to deliver a project-based assignment.

---

### **10. Content Description**
This microcredential introduces the core principles, terminology, and lifecycle of project management. Learners will explore and compare **traditional (Waterfall) and modern (Agile)** approaches to project management. Through a hands-on project, they will apply these fundamentals to initiate, plan, execute, and close a simple project.

The curriculum is structured around key project management knowledge areas and includes a dedicated module on **Agile & Scrum** methodologies. The course concludes with a forward-looking lesson on the impact of **Artificial Intelligence** on the future of project management, preparing learners for entry-level project roles and foundational industry certifications.

### **14. Competency Frameworks**
The learning outcomes map to the following skills and competencies identified through trend analysis and the European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations (ESCO) framework.

*   **LO1: Compare Waterfall & Agile lifecycles.**
    *   **Primary:** `Project management` (ESCO), `Agile development` (ESCO)
    *   **Secondary:** `Analytical Thinking` (General Competency)
*   **LO2: Apply core Agile & Scrum concepts.**
    *   **Primary:** `Agile & Scrum Methodologies` (Trends Analysis), `Scrum` (ESCO)
    *   **Secondary:** `Requirements engineering` (ESCO)
*   **LO3: Develop a project charter and scope statement.**
    *   **Primary:** `Scope Management` (Trends Analysis), `Develop a business case` (ESCO)
*   **LO4: Create a WBS and project schedule.**
    *   **Primary:** `Project Planning & Scheduling` (Trends Analysis)
    *   **Secondary:** `Use planning techniques` (ESCO)
*   **LO5: Apply risk and stakeholder management techniques.**
    *   **Primary:** `Risk Management` (Trends Analysis), `Stakeholder Communication & Management` (Trends Analysis)
    *   **Secondary:** `Manage relationships with stakeholders` (ESCO)
*   **LO6: Collaborate effectively in a team.**
    *   **Primary:** `Work in teams` (ESCO)
    *   **Secondary:** `Communication` (Trends Analysis), `Use digital collaboration tools` (Derived)

### **15. Learning Activities**
This microcredential employs a blend of `Active`, `Project-Based`, and `Inquiry-Based` learning. Learners will engage with interactive content, analyze real-world case studies, and work collaboratively on a capstone project that simulates the entire project lifecycle. The capstone project allows learners to apply either a traditional or an Agile framework, promoting critical thinking about which methodology best fits a given scenario.

### **18. Assessment Methods**
*   **Formative Quizzes (20%):** To check understanding of key concepts in both traditional and Agile approaches.
*   **Case Study Analysis (20%):** To apply knowledge to inquiry-based scenarios.
*   **Capstone Project (60%):** A team-based project requiring the submission of key project documents based on a chosen methodology (e.g., WBS/Gantt for Traditional, or Product Backlog/Sprint Plan for Agile) and a final project report/presentation.
