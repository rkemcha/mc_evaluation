### 8. Admission Requirements

### 9. Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
-   Critically evaluate a diverse range of credentials, including non-traditional and digital formats.
-   Utilize digital platforms and tools to verify and assess credential authenticity and value.
-   Apply established and emerging frameworks for determining the equivalency of alternative credentials.
-   Formulate comprehensive evaluation reports for both academic and alternative credentials that are clear, concise, and defensible.

### 10. Content Description
-   **Syllabus Outline:** The curriculum is structured into five modules:
    -   **Module 1: Foundations of Credential Evaluation:** A review of core principles, professional ethics, and research methodologies.
    -   **Module 2: Advanced Analysis of Traditional Credentials:** In-depth analysis of complex case studies, including forged documents and unconventional grading systems.
    -   **Module 3: The Alternative Credential Ecosystem:** A deep dive into microcredentials, digital badges, MOOC certificates, and industry certifications, including frameworks for their evaluation.
    -   **Module 4: Digital Tools and Platforms:** Hands-on labs in a sandbox environment for a leading credential verification platform, exploring digital wallets and verifiable credentials.
    -   **Module 5: Capstone Project:** Application of all learned skills to a mixed portfolio of traditional and alternative credentials.
