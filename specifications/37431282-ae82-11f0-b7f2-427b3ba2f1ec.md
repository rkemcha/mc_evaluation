### **8. Admission Requirements**
*   Proficiency in a high-level programming language (Python strongly recommended).
*   Demonstrable knowledge of foundational Machine Learning concepts (e.g., regression, classification, clustering).
*   An undergraduate-level understanding of educational or psychological theories related to learning and collaboration.

### **9. Learning Objectives**
Upon successful completion, the learner will be able to:
*   **LO1**: Analyze existing CSCL platforms to identify opportunities for **Generative AI** integration.
*   **LO2**: Design an AI-powered feature leveraging **generative models** to address a specific challenge in collaborative learning (e.g., free-riding, group moderation, idea synthesis).
*   **LO3**: Develop a functional prototype of a **Generative AI** tool for a CSCL environment using relevant libraries and APIs.
*   **LO4**: Evaluate the effectiveness of an AI-driven CSCL intervention based on established pedagogical frameworks and user data.
*   **LO5**: Critically analyze and mitigate the ethical implications, including bias and fairness, of using **Generative AI** in collaborative learning environments.

### **10. Content Description**
An expert-level microcredential exploring the application of artificial intelligence to enhance computer-supported collaborative learning (CSCL) environments. Learners will design, develop, and evaluate cutting-edge tools, with a focus on **Generative AI**, for group learning through hands-on, project-based labs. The curriculum covers analysis of existing platforms, design of AI-powered features, prototype development using modern libraries, and evaluation of AI interventions, with a strong emphasis on the ethical implications of using AI in education.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
*   **Delivery Mode**: Hybrid/Online. Instruction is delivered through a blend of interactive online seminars, hands-on coding labs, and collaborative project work.

*   **Generative AI Prototyping & Development**
