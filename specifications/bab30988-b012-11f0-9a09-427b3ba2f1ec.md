### **9. Learning Objectives**
- The objective of this microcredential is to equip learners with the advanced skills to:
    - Understand the theoretical and technical foundations of digital learning recognition.
    - Critically evaluate and select appropriate technologies for credentialing systems.
    - Master the process of designing a complete system architecture for recognizing learning outcomes.
    - Gain practical experience in prototyping key components of a digital credentialing system.
    - Develop strategic plans for the ethical and effective implementation and quality assurance of such systems.

---

### **10. Content Description**
- This microcredential is structured into four distinct modules that guide the learner from foundational theory to practical application.
    - **Module 1: Foundations of Learning Recognition (20 hrs):** Covers credentialing theories, Competency-Based Education, and technical standards like Open Badges and LTI.
    - **Module 2: Technology & Platform Analysis (20 hrs):** Involves a critical review of existing LMS, badging platforms, blockchain solutions, and ePortfolios.
    - **Module 3: System Design & Architecture (35 hrs):** Focuses on UX/UI for learners, Data Strategy & Analytics (Data Models, Key Metrics, Privacy, Dashboards), API integration, and security.
    - **Module 4: Capstone Project: Prototyping & Implementation (35 hrs):** Entails project scoping, agile development, creation of a functional prototype and analytics dashboard mockup, and a final presentation.

---

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
- **Mode:** Blended / Online
- **Description:** The course combines asynchronous online content (readings, pre-recorded video lectures) with synchronous live sessions (interactive workshops, Q&A, and project reviews) to provide a flexible yet engaging learning experience.

---

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
- Upon successful completion of this microcredential, the learner will be able to:
    1.  **Analyze** theoretical frameworks and technical standards (e.g., Open Badges, CLR) for recognizing learning outcomes.
    2.  **Evaluate** existing digital tools and platforms for their capacity to capture, assess, and validate learning.
    3.  **Design** a comprehensive system architecture for a digital credentialing solution, including a data model for learning analytics, tailored to a specific educational context.
    4.  **Create** a functional prototype of a digital system component for issuing or displaying a recognized learning outcome.
    5.  **Develop** a quality assurance and implementation plan, incorporating strategies for ethical data management and reporting, for deploying a learning outcome recognition system effectively.

### **18. Assessment Methods**
- Assessment is 100% based on coursework and authentic projects, with no final exam.
    - **Case Study Analysis (30%):** A written evaluation of an existing digital credentialing system.
    - **System Design Document (30%):** A detailed architectural plan for the capstone project, which must include a dedicated section on the system's data and analytics strategy.
    - **Prototype & Final Presentation (40%):** A live demonstration of the functional prototype and a defense of all design choices.
- *Provisions for assessment accommodations for learners with disabilities are available upon request through the university's disability support services.*
