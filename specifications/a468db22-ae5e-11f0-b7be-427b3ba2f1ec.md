### **9. Learning Objectives**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
1.  **Analyze** the core principles, ethical standards, and legal frameworks governing international credential evaluation.
2.  **Utilize** authoritative digital databases and research methodologies to verify the accreditation of institutions and the authenticity of qualifications.
3.  **Compare** the structure and grading systems of major global education systems to determine appropriate academic equivalencies.
4.  **Construct** a comprehensive credential evaluation report that synthesizes research, adheres to professional formatting standards, and articulates a defensible equivalency recommendation.

### **10. Content Description**
The microcredential consists of three modules designed to build skills progressively:
-   **Module 1: Foundations of Credential Evaluation (20 hours):** Introduces the core principles, ethical codes of practice, major world education systems, and the fundamental structure of a professional evaluation report.
-   **Module 2: The Evaluator's Toolkit: Research and Verification (25 hours):** Provides hands-on training with authoritative databases, transcript interpretation, and techniques for fraud detection, including a gamified "Fraudulent Document Challenge."
-   **Module 3: Practical Application: Case Studies and Reporting (30 hours):** A capstone module where learners apply all acquired skills to a portfolio of real-world case studies, culminating in the construction of complete, professional evaluation reports.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
- **Fully Online:** Delivered asynchronously to provide flexibility for working professionals, with optional synchronous workshops and Q&A sessions that are recorded for later access.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
Upon completion, the learner will have demonstrated the following competences:
-   **Critical Analysis:** Systematically deconstructing and evaluating complex information from diverse educational contexts.
-   **Digital Research & Literacy:** Efficiently navigating and vetting information from online databases, institutional websites, and verification services.
-   **Intercultural Competence:** Understanding and respecting the nuances of different educational philosophies and systems worldwide.
-   **Ethical Judgement:** Applying professional codes of conduct to situations involving fraudulent documents or ambiguous information.
-   **Professional Communication:** Articulating complex evaluation decisions clearly and concisely in written reports.

### **18. Assessment Methods**
- **Formative Assessment (30%):**
    - Module 1 Knowledge Check Quiz (10%)
    - Module 2 Fraud Detection Challenge (20%)
- **Summative Assessment (70%):**
    - **Module 3 Case Study Portfolio:** Learners produce a portfolio of professional credential evaluation reports based on complex case studies. This portfolio is assessed via a detailed rubric that explicitly weights the quality of the research, the justification of the equivalency recommendation, and the clarity and professionalism of the written report.
