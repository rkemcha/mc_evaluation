### **8. Admission Requirements**
*   A bachelor's degree in Engineering, Occupational Safety, or a related field, and a minimum of 5 years of professional experience in an occupational health and safety role.

### **9. Learning Objectives**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
1.  **Critically evaluate** the legal, technical, and financial implications of new national occupational safety standards in comparison to previous regulations.
2.  **Design** and implement advanced risk assessment and control strategies that are fully compliant with the new standards for high-hazard environments.
3.  **Formulate** a strategic plan to integrate the new standards into an existing Safety Management System (SMS), such as ISO 45001.
4.  **Lead** organizational change by developing and championing a data-informed safety culture initiative that embeds the principles of the new standards across all levels of the workforce.
5.  **Synthesize** all learned competencies into a comprehensive, board-level implementation proposal for a specific industrial case study, substantiated with key performance indicators (KPIs) and predictive analytics.

### **10. Content Description**
The curriculum is structured into four modules:
*   **Module 1: Deconstructing the New Standards: Legal and Strategic Analysis (4 ECTS):** A deep dive into the legal architecture, intent, and strategic implications of the new standards.
*   **Module 2: Advanced Risk Management Under the New Framework (6 ECTS):** Focuses on the practical application of standards to hazard identification, risk assessment (HAZOP, LOPA), and control in complex industrial settings, including hands-on labs.
*   **Module 3: Integrating Standards into Safety Management Systems & Culture (6 ECTS):** Covers the systemic and human elements of implementation, including SMS auditing, change management, and data-driven safety management using performance dashboards and predictive analytics.
*   **Module 4: Capstone Project - Strategic Implementation Plan (4 ECTS):** A culminating project where learners develop a comprehensive, board-ready plan to implement the new standards, including financial planning, a phased roadmap, and a final oral defense.
