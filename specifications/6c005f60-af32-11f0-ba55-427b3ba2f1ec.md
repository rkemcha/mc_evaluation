## 8. Admission Requirements:
A bachelor's degree in environmental science, engineering, chemistry, biology, geology, or a related scientific/technical field; or equivalent professional experience. Basic understanding of environmental principles, scientific methodology, and introductory statistics.

## 9. Learning Objectives:
*   Design comprehensive and statistically sound environmental sampling strategies across various media (e.g., water, soil, air, biota), **including strategies for emerging contaminants like PFAS**.
*   Select and apply appropriate sampling equipment and methodologies, adhering to regulatory standards and best practices, **with a focus on minimizing cross-contamination and ensuring trace-level detection for challenging analytes such as PFAS**.
*   Implement robust Quality Assurance/Quality Control (QA/QC) protocols to ensure the integrity, defensibility, and reliability of environmental data, **particularly for highly sensitive analyses required for emerging contaminants**.
*   Utilize advanced analytical and statistical tools for the interpretation and visualization of complex environmental sampling data, **including techniques for assessing uncertainty in trace contaminant analysis**.
*   Critically evaluate and mitigate sources of bias, uncertainty, and error in environmental sampling campaigns, **with specific attention to the unique challenges posed by emerging contaminants like PFAS**.
*   Communicate sampling plans, data analysis, and findings effectively to diverse technical and non-technical stakeholders, **addressing the specific concerns and regulatory implications of contaminants like PFAS**.

## 10. Content Description:
The microcredential is structured into modules covering the full lifecycle of advanced environmental sampling. Content has been updated to integrate the trend of PFAS Contamination Analysis across relevant sections.

*   **Module 1: Foundations of Strategic Sampling Design**
    *   Review of core sampling principles and statistical considerations.
    *   Advanced spatial and temporal sampling designs.
    *   New section: **Sampling Design for Emerging Contaminants (e.g., PFAS)**, focusing on unique challenges and objectives.
*   **Module 2: Advanced Field Sampling Techniques**
    *   Selection and deployment of specialized sampling equipment for various media.
    *   Advanced quality control in the field.
    *   New section: **Specialized Sampling Protocols for Trace Contaminants (e.g., PFAS-free sampling)**, emphasizing materials compatibility and cross-contamination prevention.
*   **Module 3: Quality Assurance/Quality Control (QA/QC) for Environmental Data**
    *   Design and implementation of robust QA/QC programs.
    *   Data defensibility and reliability metrics.
    *   Enhanced section: **Advanced QA/QC for Trace Organic Contaminants and Method Detection Limits (MDLs), with PFAS examples.**
*   **Module 4: Environmental Data Analysis and Interpretation**
    *   Application of advanced statistical and geospatial tools.
    *   Data visualization techniques for complex datasets.
    *   Case study: **Interpreting Complex PFAS Datasets and Trend Analysis.**
*   **Module 5: Risk, Uncertainty, and Communication in Sampling Campaigns**
    *   Critical evaluation and mitigation of bias, uncertainty, and error.
    *   Effective communication to diverse stakeholders.
    *   Case study: **Assessing Uncertainty in PFAS Exposure and Remediation Data.**
    *   Discussion on communicating regulatory implications and specific concerns of contaminants like PFAS.

## 15. Learning Activities:
*   **Capstone Project**: Design and present a comprehensive environmental sampling plan for a real-world scenario. Learners can *choose* or be assigned a scenario that involves **designing a PFAS investigation and monitoring plan** for a contaminated site, including specialized QA/QC and data interpretation.
*   **Case Study Analyses**: Critical evaluation of existing environmental sampling projects. Include a case study focusing on a real-world **PFAS contamination event**, requiring learners to critically evaluate the sampling strategy, data integrity issues, and communication.
*   **Practical Exercises/Simulations**: Hands-on activities or virtual simulations focused on equipment selection, sampling protocols, and data collection. Include virtual simulations or scenario-based exercises demonstrating **PFAS-free sampling techniques** and challenges in preventing cross-contamination during field work.
*   **Quizzes/Assignments**: Short assessments to test understanding of theoretical concepts and regulatory requirements.
