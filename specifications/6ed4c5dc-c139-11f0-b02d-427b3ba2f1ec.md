### **8. Admission Requirements**

### **9. Learning Objectives**
This microcredential provides learners with advanced, practical skills in international arbitration advocacy. It is designed to bridge the gap between theoretical legal knowledge and the high-stakes performance required in global dispute resolution.

### **10. Content Description**
The course is structured into five progressive modules, blending asynchronous learning with live, interactive workshops.

*   **Module 1: Foundations of Arbitration Advocacy & Case Strategy**
    *   Topics: Introduction to international arbitration advocacy, developing case theory, procedural strategy, and team assembly.
*   **Module 2: The Written Record: Persuasive Drafting**
    *   Topics: Drafting Requests for Arbitration and Answers, managing written evidence, and effective document production.
*   **Module 3: The Hearing: Mastering Oral Advocacy**
    *   Topics: Crafting opening statements, techniques for direct and cross-examination, use of expert witnesses, and delivering closing arguments.
*   **Module 4: Advanced Topics: Technology, Experts, and Ethics**
    *   Topics: Ethical use of technology and Generative AI in legal research and drafting, advanced hearing techniques.
*   **Module 5: Capstone: Mock Arbitration Hearing & Reflection**
    *   Topics: A full-scale simulated hearing where learners perform as counsel, followed by a debrief and the completion of a reflective analysis.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
*   **Blended Learning:** A combination of asynchronous online learning (pre-recorded lectures, readings) and synchronous online workshops and simulations.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
Upon successful completion, learners will be able to:
1.  Critically evaluate and formulate a cohesive case strategy for an international arbitration proceeding.
2.  Draft persuasive and procedurally compliant written submissions, such as requests for arbitration and statements of case.
3.  Execute effective oral advocacy techniques for opening statements, witness examination, and closing arguments.
4.  Analyze and integrate advanced concepts, including the ethical use of technology and AI, into their advocacy practice.
5.  Synthesize theoretical knowledge and practical skills through performance in a simulated arbitration hearing.

### **15. Learning Activities**
*   **Asynchronous Learning:** Engagement with pre-recorded video lectures, foundational readings, and influential articles.
*   **Live Synchronous Workshops:** Interactive sessions via video conference focusing on case study analysis, peer review of draft submissions, and practical drills (e.g., cross-examination exercises).
*   **Collaborative Case Strategy:** Team-based sessions to develop case strategy using collaborative tools. An accessible, text-based template (e.g., in Google Docs) is provided as an alternative to visual whiteboard tools like Miro.
*   **Mock Arbitration Hearing:** A capstone simulation where learners perform advocacy roles in a realistic hearing environment.
*   **Reflective Practice:** A written reflection connecting the practical simulation experience with theoretical concepts.
