## 9. Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
*   **LO1:** **Understand** the fundamental concepts of Artificial Intelligence and its specific applications for data analysis in educational settings.
*   **LO2:** **Identify** and **differentiate** between various AI tools and techniques suitable for interpreting educational data and supporting data-driven decision-making.
*   **LO3:** **Apply** basic AI principles to analyze simplified educational datasets, deriving preliminary insights and informing instructional strategies.
*   **LO4:** **Evaluate** the ethical considerations, biases, and limitations associated with deploying AI for data analysis in educational technologies.

## 10. Content Description

### Module 1: Introduction to AI in Educational Data (Approx. 30 hours)
*   **Topic:** What is AI? Types of AI relevant to data analysis. Introduction to educational data types (learning analytics, assessment data, behavioral data). The potential of AI for data analysis in EdTech.
*   **LOs Addressed:** LO1
*   **Reasoning:** Establishes foundational knowledge and context.

### Module 2: Core AI Techniques for Educational Data Analysis (Approx. 35 hours)
*   **Topic:** Overview of key AI techniques: Machine Learning (supervised vs. unsupervised), Natural Language Processing (NLP) basics for text data, Predictive Analytics. Focus on conceptual understanding and recognizing applications rather than coding.
*   **LOs Addressed:** LO1, LO2
*   **Reasoning:** Introduces specific AI methods relevant to educational data without requiring deep technical expertise.

### Module 3: Applying AI Tools for Educational Insights (Approx. 35 hours)
*   **Topic:** Introduction to user-friendly AI tools and platforms (e.g., data visualization tools, basic online ML platforms, or spreadsheet-based AI features). Hands-on exercises with simulated or anonymized educational datasets to perform basic analysis and extract insights.
*   **LOs Addressed:** LO2, LO3
*   **Reasoning:** Provides practical application of concepts using accessible tools, fulfilling the "apply" LO.

### Module 4: Ethical Considerations & Future Trends (Approx. 25 hours)
*   **Topic:** Data privacy (GDPR, FERPA), algorithmic bias, explainable AI (XAI) in education, equitable use of AI, human-AI collaboration. Emerging trends and the future of AI in educational data analysis.
*   **LOs Addressed:** LO4
*   **Reasoning:** Addresses critical ethical and societal impacts, ensuring responsible application of AI.

## 11. Mode of Instruction
Online (asynchronous with optional synchronous sessions for Q&A/discussions)

## 12. Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
*   **LO1: Understand AI Fundamentals:** Grasp the basic definitions, principles, and historical context of Artificial Intelligence, including its various subfields and approaches, specifically in relation to data analysis in educational settings.
*   **LO2: Identify and Differentiate AI Tools & Techniques:** Recognize and distinguish between various AI tools and techniques (e.g., classification, clustering, NLP) based on their principles, applications, strengths, and weaknesses for interpreting educational data and supporting data-driven decision-making.
*   **LO3: Apply Basic AI for Educational Data Analysis:** Utilize user-friendly AI tools or platforms to perform fundamental analytical tasks on simplified educational datasets, derive preliminary insights, and translate these into actionable instructional strategies.
*   **LO4: Evaluate Ethical AI Deployment in Education:** Critically assess the ethical considerations, potential biases, and inherent limitations of AI tools and techniques when deployed for data analysis within educational technologies, including data privacy and algorithmic fairness.

## 18. Assessment Methods
*   **Weekly Gamified Quizzes/Challenges (Formative, 20%):** Short, interactive quizzes and mini-challenges within each module to test understanding and reinforce concepts, contributing to a "learning score."
*   **Case Study Analysis (Summative, 30%):** Learners will analyze a provided educational data scenario, identify potential AI applications, and propose data-driven solutions (LO1, LO2, LO4).
*   **Practical Application Project (Summative, 40%):** A final project where learners use a simplified AI tool (e.g., basic spreadsheet functions, visual analytics platforms, or a simple online AI model) to analyze a small educational dataset and present their findings and insights (LO2, LO3).
*   **Active Participation & Discussion (Formative, 10%):** Engagement in online forums, peer feedback, and collaborative activities.
*   *Rubrics will be provided for all summative assessments to ensure transparent and consistent grading.*
