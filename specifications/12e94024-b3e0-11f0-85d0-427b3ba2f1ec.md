## 10. Content Description
This foundational microcredential provides an essential introduction to microcredentials, focusing specifically on their application and impact within Vocational Education and Training (VET). Learners will explore the core concepts, benefits, and challenges of microcredentials, understand their relevance for upskilling and reskilling in the VET sector, and gain practical insights into their design, implementation, and quality assurance. The course emphasizes a blended learning approach, combining online resources with practical exercises to equip participants with the knowledge to navigate and contribute to the evolving landscape of VET qualifications.

The microcredential is structured into three modules:

*   **Module 1: Foundations of Microcredentials (Understanding the Microcredential Landscape)**
    *   Introduction to Microcredentials: Definitions, characteristics, types.
    *   The "Why" of Microcredentials: Drivers, benefits, challenges.
    *   Global Landscape and Policy: International examples, emerging frameworks.

*   **Module 2: Microcredentials in Vocational Education and Training (VET) (Strategic Integration of Microcredentials in VET)**
    *   VET Sector Specifics: Challenges and opportunities.
    *   Microcredentials as a Solution: Addressing skill gaps, industry relevance, stackability.
    *   Alignment with Industrial Certifications: Complementing existing VET qualifications.
    *   Case Studies: Successful VET microcredential models.
    *   Stakeholder Perspectives: Employers, learners, VET institutions, government.

*   **Module 3: Designing and Implementing VET Microcredentials (Practical Design and Quality Assurance for VET Microcredentials)**
    *   Curriculum Design Principles: Backward design, competency-based approaches.
    *   Developing Learning Outcomes: SMART outcomes, aligning with industry standards.
    *   Content Curation and Development: Digital resources, practical components.
    *   Assessment Strategies: Formative vs. summative, authentic assessment.
    *   Quality Assurance and Accreditation: Standards, verification, digital badging.
    *   Implementation Strategies: Marketing, learner support, technology platforms.

## 18. Assessment Methods
The microcredential employs a balanced assessment strategy combining formative and summative elements:

*   **Formative Assessments (do not contribute to final grade):**
    *   Participation in online discussions
    *   Completion of interactive quizzes
    *   Peer feedback sessions on draft project work
*   **Summative Assessments (contribute to final grade):**
    *   **Module 1:** Online Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQ) and Short Answer Quiz (20% of final grade). *Assesses LO1.*
    *   **Module 2:** Case Study Analysis Report (30% of final grade). *Assesses LO2, LO5.*
    *   **Module 3:** **Microcredential Design Project** (50% of final grade). Learners will submit a detailed outline for a new VET microcredential, including justification, learning outcomes, content structure, learning activities, assessment strategy, and quality assurance considerations. *Assesses LO3, LO4, LO5.*

*   **Certification Requirement:** A minimum passing grade of 60% across all summative assessments is required for certification.
*   **Recommendation:** Detailed assessment rubrics will be developed for the Case Study Analysis Report and the Microcredential Design Project to ensure consistent and objective grading. A moderation process will be implemented for summative assessments to enhance reliability.
