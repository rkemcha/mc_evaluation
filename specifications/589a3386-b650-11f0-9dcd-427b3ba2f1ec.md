## 9. Learning Objectives
**Defined Educational Goals:**
This microcredential aims to equip educational professionals with foundational knowledge and practical skills to leverage AI for data analysis support. Upon completion, learners will be able to:
*   Understand core AI concepts relevant to data analysis.
*   Identify various types of educational data and their analytical potential.
*   Apply basic no-code/low-code AI tools for data exploration and analysis.
*   Critically and ethically interpret AI-driven analysis results in educational contexts.
*   Propose AI applications in education and communicate insights effectively to stakeholders.

## 10. Content Description
**Detailed Modules/Content:**
The microcredential is structured into four modules and a final project, totaling 135 hours over 5 ECTS.

**Overall Workload Distribution (135 hours total):**
*   Module 1: 25 hours
*   Module 2: 30 hours
*   Module 3: 35 hours
*   Module 4: 35 hours
*   Final Project/Assessment: 10 hours

**Module Breakdown:**

*   **Module 1: Introduction to AI and Educational Data (25 hours)**
    *   **Topics:** What is AI? (ML, DL, NLP overview), AI in Education landscape, Types of Educational Data, Ethical considerations of AI in education, The concept of AI democratization and no-code/low-code AI.
    *   **Description:** This module provides a foundational understanding of AI, its relevance in education, the diverse nature of educational data, and introduces the ethical dimensions of AI use. It sets the stage for leveraging accessible AI tools.

*   **Module 2: Data Preparation and Exploration for AI (30 hours)**
    *   **Topics:** Data collection principles, Data cleaning and preprocessing basics, Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) with visual tools, Feature identification in educational data, Using no-code/low-code platform features for data import and basic preparation.
    *   **Description:** Learners will acquire essential skills in preparing and exploring educational datasets. This module focuses on practical data handling techniques using accessible tools, ensuring data is ready for AI analysis.

*   **Module 3: Basic AI Tools for Educational Insights (35 hours)**
    *   **Topics:** Introduction to no-code/low-code AI platforms (e.g., Google AutoML, Microsoft Azure ML Studio, educational-specific tools), Applying basic classification/regression models, Clustering for student grouping, Natural Language Processing (NLP) for text analysis (e.g., feedback analysis), Practical application through guided exercises and mini-projects on these platforms.
    *   **Description:** This is the core application module, where learners get hands-on experience using user-friendly AI platforms to perform actual data analysis on educational data, directly addressing the microcredential's focus on AI for data analysis support.

*   **Module 4: Interpreting Results and Ethical AI in Education (35 hours)**
    *   **Topics:** Understanding model outputs (accuracy, precision, recall), Bias and fairness in AI models, Privacy and security of educational data, Communicating AI insights to stakeholders (Data Visualization & Storytelling), Future trends of AI in education, Strategies for responsible implementation of AI tools in educational settings.
    *   **Description:** This module moves beyond application to critical evaluation. Learners will interpret AI model outputs, understand their limitations, and delve into the crucial ethical and privacy considerations unique to educational data. Emphasis is placed on communicating insights effectively to stakeholders.

## 12. Learning Outcomes
**Skills/Knowledge/Competencies Achieved:**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:

*   **LO1: Understand foundational AI concepts:** Explain core AI concepts, including machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing, relevant to data analysis.
*   **LO2: Identify educational data types:** Recognize and differentiate various types of educational data (e.g., student performance, engagement, administrative) and their potential for analysis.
*   **LO3: Apply basic AI tools for data exploration and analysis:** Use introductory **no-code/low-code** AI-powered tools and platforms to explore, visualize, preprocess, and apply basic analytical models to educational datasets.
*   **LO4: Interpret AI-driven analysis results critically and ethically:** Critically evaluate and interpret the insights generated by AI models in educational contexts, understanding their limitations, **potential biases, and ethical implications.**
*   **LO5: Propose AI applications in education and communicate insights effectively:** Formulate basic proposals for how AI can support data-driven decision-making and improve educational outcomes, and **effectively communicate AI-derived insights to diverse educational stakeholders.**

## 15. Learning Activities
**Teaching/Practical Activities:**
To align with "Active Learning," "Gamification," and "Project-Based Learning," activities are designed to be interactive, problem-oriented, and provide immediate feedback, with a strong emphasis on the **Democratization of AI through No-code/Low-code Platforms.**

*   **Interactive Lectures/Videos:** Short, focused content delivery with embedded quizzes.
*   **Case Studies:** Analysis of real-world educational scenarios where AI can be applied, fostering critical thinking.
*   **Hands-on Tool Exercises:** Guided tutorials and practice sessions using **no-code/low-code AI platforms** with sample educational datasets, focusing on practical application.
*   **Discussion Forums:** Peer-to-peer learning and discussion on ethical dilemmas, interpretations of results, or potential applications.
*   **Gamified Quizzes/Challenges:** Short, interactive quizzes with immediate feedback, points, and leaderboards to reinforce concepts.
*   **Mini-Projects/Data Sprints:** Small, focused data analysis tasks within each module, building towards the final project, **utilizing no-code/low-code AI tools.**
*   **Peer Review:** Opportunities for learners to review and provide feedback on each other's mini-project outputs or proposals, **including feedback on ethical considerations and clarity of communication.**

## 18. Assessment Methods
**Evaluation Techniques:**
Assessments are chosen to directly measure the learning outcomes and are aligned with the active and project-based learning approaches, particularly the application of **no-code/low-code AI platforms.**

*   **Module Quizzes (30%):** Short, multiple-choice or short-answer quizzes at the end of each module to assess understanding of core concepts (LO1, LO2, LO4). These can be gamified.
    *   **Criteria:** Accuracy of answers, demonstration of conceptual understanding.
*   **Hands-on Tool Assignments (40%):** Practical assignments where learners apply **no-code/low-code AI platforms** to analyze provided educational datasets, demonstrating their ability to use tools and interpret basic results (LO3, LO4).
    *   **Criteria:** Correct application of **no-code/low-code AI platforms**, accurate data preprocessing, meaningful visualization, logical interpretation of results, clear explanation of steps taken, **consideration of ethical implications in tool usage.**
*   **Mini-Project/Proposal (30%):** A cumulative project where learners propose an AI solution for a specific educational problem, outline data needs, explain how AI could support analysis **using accessible tools, and effectively communicate their findings**, demonstrating synthesis and application (LO1, LO2, LO4, LO5).
    *   **Criteria:** Clarity and relevance of the proposed AI application, logical structure, feasibility of data acquisition and analysis, **demonstration of responsible AI considerations (e.g., bias, privacy), clear articulation and effective communication of potential impact to stakeholders.**

**Grading Scale:** Pass/Fail (with a minimum overall score of 70% to pass).
