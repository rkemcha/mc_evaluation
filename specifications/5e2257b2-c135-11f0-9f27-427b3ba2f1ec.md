### **9. Learning Objectives**
*   This microcredential provides advanced, practical training in the core skills of advocacy specific to the context of international arbitration. It is designed for legal professionals who wish to develop the strategic, drafting, and oral presentation skills necessary to represent clients effectively in complex international disputes. The course integrates cutting-edge legal technology, focusing on the ethical and strategic use of Generative AI in modern advocacy.

### **10. Content Description**
The curriculum is structured into four modules that follow the lifecycle of an arbitration case:
*   **Module 1: Foundations & Case Strategy (Weeks 1-2):** Establishes the theoretical framework, procedural options, and strategic thinking required to build a cohesive case theory from the outset.
*   **Module 2: The Art of Written Advocacy (Weeks 3-5):** Focuses on drafting persuasive and compliant written submissions, including memorials, counter-memorials, and witness statements.
*   **Module 3: Oral Advocacy & Witness Handling (Weeks 6-8):** A practical, performance-focused module covering opening/closing statements, and the techniques of direct and cross-examination in a hearing setting.
*   **Module 4: Advanced Topics, Ethics, and Technology (Weeks 9-10):** Explores the ethical duties of counsel in international arbitration and critically examines the impact of emerging technologies, particularly Generative AI, on advocacy techniques.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
*   **Blended Learning:** The course combines asynchronous online learning with mandatory synchronous sessions.
    *   **Asynchronous:** Pre-recorded lectures, curated readings, and self-paced practical exercises via the university's LMS.
    *   **Synchronous:** Weekly interactive workshops and mock hearings conducted via video conference.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, the learner will be able to:
1.  Formulate a cohesive case strategy for an international arbitration proceeding.
2.  Draft persuasive written submissions, including memorials and witness statements, compliant with international standards.
3.  Conduct effective oral advocacy, including opening/closing statements and witness examination/cross-examination.
4.  Critically analyze and apply ethical principles specific to international arbitration.
5.  Leverage Generative AI tools to enhance legal research, drafting, and case analysis while critically evaluating their output for accuracy, bias, and strategic utility.
6.  Evaluate the impact of emerging technologies and research on advocacy techniques in the field.

### **13. Level of Experience**
*   **Advanced:** Aligned with EQF Level 7/8. This microcredential requires learners to have a foundational understanding of law and to engage in complex analysis, synthesis, and practical application.

### **14. Competency Frameworks**
This microcredential develops the following core professional competencies:
*   **Case Strategy Development:** Synthesizing facts, law, and procedure into a coherent plan of action.
*   **Written Advocacy:** Using written communication to persuade in a formal legal context.
*   **Oral Advocacy:** Structuring and delivering persuasive legal arguments verbally and examining witnesses.
*   **Arbitration Ethics:** Applying the professional and regulatory framework governing an advocate's conduct in international arbitration.
*   **Critical AI Literacy in Law:** Using AI tools with a skeptical and analytical mindset to enhance legal practice.
*   **Legal Trend Analysis:** Evaluating emerging technological and research developments to anticipate the evolution of the legal profession.

### **15. Learning Activities**

### **16. Workload Theoretical**
*   **50 hours:** This includes watching asynchronous pre-recorded lectures, completing assigned readings, and independent study.

### **17. Workload Practical**
*   **75 hours:** This includes:
    *   25 hours of participation in live synchronous workshops and mock hearings.

### **18. Assessment Methods**
*   **Written Submission (40%):** Drafting of a key procedural document (e.g., Request for Arbitration or Statement of Defence). This submission must include an appendix detailing any use of AI tools, including prompts used and a critical evaluation of the generated output.
*   **Mock Arbitration Hearing (40%):** Performance in a simulated hearing, assessed on oral argument, procedural competence, and witness handling.
*   **Reflective Essay (20%):** A critical analysis of the ethical implications and strategic impact of Generative AI on the role and responsibilities of an advocate in international arbitration.
