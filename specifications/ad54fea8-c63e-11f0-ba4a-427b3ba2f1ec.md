## 9. Learning Objectives:
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:

*   **LO1:** Define core concepts of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) relevant to educational contexts.
*   **LO2:** Identify potential applications and ethical implications of AI in various educational settings.
*   **LO3:** Evaluate common biases in AI systems and propose strategies for fair and equitable AI integration in education.
*   **LO4:** Apply principles of prompt engineering to effectively utilize generative AI tools for educational content creation and instructional design.
*   **LO5:** Propose strategies for responsible data governance and privacy in educational AI applications, adhering to relevant regulations (e.g., GDPR).

## 10. Content Description:
This microcredential is structured into five modules, blending theoretical knowledge with practical application through active and project-based learning.

*   **Module 1: Introduction to AI and ML in Education (LO1)**
    *   Foundational concepts of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning.
    *   Historical overview and current trends in AI development.
    *   Exploration of specific AI/ML applications in educational technologies (e.g., adaptive learning platforms, intelligent tutoring systems, automated assessment tools, content recommendation engines).
    *   The role of AI in personalizing learning experiences and supporting educators.

*   **Module 2: Ethical AI in Educational Contexts (LO2, LO3)**
    *   Introduction to ethical principles guiding AI development and deployment (fairness, accountability, transparency, privacy, safety).
    *   Identification of potential algorithmic bias in educational data sets and AI systems, and its impact on diverse learners.
    *   Strategies for mitigating bias and promoting equitable access and outcomes in AI-powered education.
    *   Discussions on the societal implications, equity concerns, and digital divides related to AI integration in education.

*   **Module 3: Generative AI and Prompt Engineering for Educators (LO4)**
    *   Understanding the capabilities and limitations of generative AI models (e.g., Large Language Models, text-to-image generators).
    *   Fundamentals of prompt engineering: techniques for crafting clear, effective, and ethical prompts for various educational tasks.
    *   Practical application of generative AI tools for lesson planning, curriculum development, creating diverse learning materials, generating assessment items, and providing personalized feedback.
    *   Responsible use of generative AI, including intellectual property and originality considerations.

*   **Module 4: Data Governance and Privacy in EdTech AI (LO5)**
    *   Overview of key data privacy regulations relevant to education (e.g., General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) if applicable nationally, and other local data protection laws).
    *   Principles of secure data handling, data minimization, anonymization, and pseudonymization in educational settings.
    *   Strategies for obtaining informed consent from students and parents, and managing student data in AI applications.
    *   Best practices for data security and compliance within EdTech ecosystems.

*   **Module 5: Designing and Implementing AI-Enhanced Learning Experiences (Project-Based) (Integrates LO1-LO5)**
    *   Frameworks and methodologies for integrating AI tools and concepts into existing curriculum and instructional strategies.
    *   Best practices for designing, piloting, and evaluating AI-enhanced learning activities and solutions.
    *   Analysis of case studies showcasing successful and challenging AI implementations in diverse educational contexts.
    *   Focus on practical, actionable strategies for educators and instructional designers to leverage AI responsibly and effectively.

## 14. Competency Frameworks:
This microcredential aligns with and contributes to the following competency frameworks:

*   **DigComp 2.2 for Citizens:** Enhances skills in "Information and data literacy," "Communication and collaboration through digital technologies" (especially with AI tools), and "Digital content creation" (using generative AI).
*   **UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Educators:** Addresses foundational understanding of AI concepts, ethical considerations in AI use, and practical application of AI in teaching and learning processes.
*   **European Framework for the Digital Competence of Educators (DigCompEdu):** Strengthens digital competences related to "Digital Resources" (evaluating and creating AI-powered tools), "Teaching and Learning" (designing AI-enhanced activities), "Assessment" (using AI for feedback), and "Empowering Learners" (fostering responsible AI use).

## 15. Learning Activities:
*   **Interactive Online Modules:** Engaging multimedia content, including videos, expert interviews, animated explanations, and curated readings.
*   **Discussion Forums:** Facilitated asynchronous discussions for peer-to-peer learning, sharing insights, and debating ethical dilemmas in AI in education.
*   **Case Study Analysis:** Deep dives into real-world scenarios of AI implementation in education, requiring critical analysis and proposed solutions.
*   **Prompt Engineering Exercises:** Hands-on practice with generative AI tools to create various educational outputs, with iterative refinement.
*   **Collaborative Project Work:** Group-based design project to develop an AI-enhanced learning activity or solution, fostering teamwork and practical application.
*   **Self-Assessment Quizzes:** Low-stakes quizzes to check comprehension and reinforce learning throughout the modules.
*   **Reflective Journaling:** Opportunities for learners to reflect on their understanding, ethical considerations, and practical application of AI concepts.

## 18. Assessment Methods:
Assessment methods are designed to be valid, reliable, fair, transparent, and directly aligned with the stated learning outcomes and pedagogical approaches:

*   **Module Quizzes (20%):** Short, objective quizzes (multiple-choice, true/false) at the end of Modules 1, 2, and 4 to assess comprehension of core AI/ML concepts, ethical principles, and data privacy regulations (measures LO1, LO2, LO5).
*   **Ethical AI Case Study Analysis (30%):** Learners will submit a written analysis of a provided case study, identifying ethical issues, potential biases in AI systems, and proposing responsible integration strategies in an educational context (measures LO2, LO3, LO5).
*   **Prompt Engineering Portfolio (20%):** Students will create a portfolio showcasing a series of prompts and their generated outputs from generative AI tools for at least three different educational tasks, accompanied by a brief reflection on prompt effectiveness and refinement (measures LO4).
*   **Final Project: AI-Enhanced Learning Activity Design (30%):** A summative project where learners design a detailed plan for an AI-enhanced learning activity or solution for a specific educational context. The plan must include pedagogical justification, consideration of ethical implications, data privacy measures, and demonstrate effective use of AI concepts (measures LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5).
