### **9. Learning Objectives**
This microcredential provides an in-depth exploration of the technologies and principles behind Smart Cities. Learners will analyze, design, and evaluate technology-driven solutions to urban challenges such as transportation, energy management, and public services. The course emphasizes a hands-on, project-based approach, focusing on IoT, Big Data Analytics, Generative AI, and machine learning to build sustainable and efficient urban systems.

### **10. Content Description**
The curriculum is structured into five thematic modules delivered over 15 weeks:
*   **Module 1: Foundations of Smart Cities (Weeks 1-3):** Introduction to urban technologies, socio-technical systems, policy frameworks, and ethical considerations.
*   **Module 2: Urban Data Acquisition (Weeks 4-6):** Deep dive into IoT, sensor networks, and communication protocols (MQTT). Includes hands-on labs for designing and modeling sensor networks.
*   **Module 3: Urban Data Analytics & Machine Learning (Weeks 7-9):** Applying predictive modeling and data analytics to urban datasets for challenges like traffic prediction and energy optimization.
*   **Module 4: Emerging Technologies in Urban Contexts (Weeks 10-12):** Focus on cutting-edge research and application. Includes a dedicated unit on **Generative AI for Urban Solutions**, covering LLMs for sentiment analysis, urban simulation, and policy drafting. A new hands-on lab focuses on using LLM APIs (e.g., Hugging Face, OpenAI).
*   **Module 5: Capstone Project (Weeks 13-15):** Learners integrate their knowledge to develop and present a prototype solution for a real-world urban challenge.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
*   **Blended:** A combination of online asynchronous content (lectures, readings) and synchronous, instructor-led project-based labs and mentoring sessions.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, the learner will be able to:
*   **LO1:** Analyze the complex interplay of technology, policy, and society in smart city ecosystems.
*   **LO2:** Design and model IoT-based sensor networks for urban data collection.
*   **LO3:** Apply data analytics and machine learning techniques, including predictive and generative models, to urban datasets for modeling, simulation, and decision support.
*   **LO4:** Evaluate the security, privacy, and ethical implications of smart city technologies, with specific attention to the challenges posed by AI and generative models.
*   **LO5:** Create and present a prototype solution for a specific urban challenge, integrating multiple technologies.

### **15. Learning Activities**
*   **Asynchronous Learning:** Engaging with pre-recorded video lectures, curated academic papers, and technical documentation.
*   **Synchronous Labs:** Weekly hands-on lab sessions focused on practical skills, including IoT hardware configuration, data analysis with Python, and a lab on Urban Sentiment Analysis using LLMs.
*   **Research Presentation:** Independent analysis and presentation of an emerging research paper in the smart city domain.
*   **Capstone Project:** A semester-long, project-based learning activity where learners define an urban problem, design a technical solution, build a prototype, and present their findings.

### **16. Workload Theoretical**
*   **75 Hours:** This includes time spent on asynchronous lectures, readings, and preparation for the research presentation.

### **17. Workload Practical**
*   **105 Hours:** This includes time spent in synchronous labs, hands-on development, and work on the final capstone project.
*   **Total Workload:** 180 hours (equivalent to 6 ECTS).

### **18. Assessment Methods**
*   **Lab Reports (30%):** Formative assessments of hands-on lab components, including the new lab on applying LLMs.
*   **Research Presentation (20%):** Summative assessment of the learner's ability to analyze and critique emerging research.
*   **Final Project Portfolio (50%):** Summative assessment including a functional prototype, a technical report, and a final presentation. The project rubric includes criteria for the innovative use of emerging technologies, such as Generative AI, to enhance the solution.
