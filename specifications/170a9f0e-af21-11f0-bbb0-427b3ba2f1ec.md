# Microcredential Specification: Foundations of Applied AI

### **Executive Summary**

This document outlines the complete specification for the **Foundations of Applied AI** microcredential. It is designed as a university-level module at **EQF Level 6**, awarding **6 ECTS** credits based on a total workload of **150 hours**. The program is delivered in English and employs a blended, project-based learning approach to equip learners with foundational skills in Python programming, machine learning concepts, and responsible AI practices. This specification is structured to meet European standards for quality, transparency, and recognition.

### **9. Learning Objectives**

This microcredential aims to provide learners with the foundational knowledge and practical skills to:
*   Understand and articulate the core concepts of modern Artificial Intelligence and machine learning.
*   Develop technical proficiency in using Python for data analysis and visualization.
*   Gain hands-on experience with the AI project lifecycle, from conception to evaluation.
*   Explore the capabilities and limitations of Generative AI.
*   Formulate and apply ethical principles for responsible AI development.

### **10. Content Description**

The curriculum is structured across six modules in a logical progression:

*   **Module 1: Foundations of AI & Python Programming:** Introduction to AI, history, and key terminology. Hands-on Python essentials, NumPy for numerical data, and Pandas for data manipulation.
*   **Module 2: Core Machine Learning Concepts:** Supervised Learning (Regression, Classification), Unsupervised Learning (Clustering). Model training and evaluation metrics.
*   **Module 3: The AI Project Lifecycle & Data Visualization:** From business understanding to data preparation and modeling. Using Matplotlib and Seaborn for effective data storytelling.
*   **Module 4: Introduction to Generative AI & LLMs:** Understanding transformers, Large Language Models (LLMs), and their applications. Practical prompt engineering techniques and limitations.
*   **Module 5: AI Ethics & Responsible Innovation:** Bias in data, fairness, accountability, and transparency in AI models. Frameworks for responsible AI.
*   **Module 6: Capstone Project:** A culminating project where learners apply all learned skills to analyze a novel dataset, build a predictive model, and present their findings, including an ethical impact assessment.

### **11. Mode of Instruction**

*   The microcredential employs a **Blended Learning** model, combining asynchronous online lectures with synchronous, hands-on workshops and labs. The core pedagogical strategy is **Project-Based Learning**.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**

Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:

*   **LO1:** Analyze core machine learning concepts, including supervised and unsupervised learning, to select appropriate models for given problems.
*   **LO2:** Apply foundational Python libraries (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib) to perform data cleaning, manipulation, analysis, and visualization.
*   **LO3:** Evaluate the stages of the AI project lifecycle, from problem formulation and data collection to model evaluation.
*   **LO4:** Design effective prompts and critique the outputs of Generative AI and Large Language Models (LLMs) for specific tasks.
*   **LO5:** Justify the integration of ethical principles and responsible AI practices into the design and deployment of AI systems.
*   **LO6:** Autonomously manage and execute a capstone project that involves analyzing a dataset, applying a machine learning model, and communicating the results and ethical implications.

### **18. Assessment Methods**

*   **Portfolio of Projects (60%):** Consists of 4-5 practical assignments from Modules 1-4 that demonstrate proficiency in Python, data analysis, and ML modeling.
*   **Knowledge Check Quizzes (15%):** Short online quizzes at the end of Modules 1-5 to assess understanding of core concepts.
*   **Final Capstone Project & Presentation (25%):** A final project requiring learners to analyze a dataset, build a model, and present their methodology, findings, and ethical considerations in a recorded presentation.

*A minimum overall grade of 50% is required to pass.*
