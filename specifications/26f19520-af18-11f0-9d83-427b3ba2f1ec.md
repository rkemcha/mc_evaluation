### **8. Admission Requirements**
- **Knowledge:** A foundational understanding of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning concepts, proficiency in Python, and familiarity with linear algebra, calculus, and probability theory are required.
- **Skills:** Demonstrable programming skills with data manipulation libraries (e.g., Pandas, NumPy) are essential for success.

### **9. Learning Objectives**
- This microcredential provides an in-depth exploration of advanced concepts and methodologies in Artificial Intelligence, with a strong focus on **Generative AI**. Learners will move beyond foundational knowledge to master complex topics such as deep learning architectures, generative models for vision, and large language models for natural language processing. The course emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical application, preparing learners to design, build, and deploy real-world AI solutions.

### **10. Content Description**
- The curriculum is structured into the following modules:
    - **Module 1:** Foundations of Deep Learning (Recap & Advanced Architectures)
    - **Module 2:** Advanced Computer Vision (Generative Models like GANs, Transformers for Vision)
    - **Module 3:** Advanced Natural Language Processing (Generative NLP with Transformers and Large Language Models)
    - **Module 4:** Reinforcement Learning and Decision Making
    - **Module 5:** AI Ethics and Responsible Innovation
    - **Module 6:** Capstone Project: Applied AI Solution

### **11. Mode of Instruction**
- **Blended Learning:** A combination of online and in-person components.

### **12. Learning Outcomes**
- Upon successful completion of this microcredential, the learner will be able to:
    - **LO1:** Analyze and critique state-of-the-art AI models and algorithms.
    - **LO2:** Design and implement complex deep learning solutions for specific problem domains (e.g., NLP, Computer Vision).
    - **LO3:** Evaluate the performance and ethical implications of advanced AI systems.
    - **LO4:** Formulate and execute an applied AI project from conception to a functional prototype, including model deployment.
    - **LO5:** Communicate complex technical AI concepts effectively to both specialist and non-specialist audiences.

### **13. Level of Experience**
- **European Qualifications Framework (EQF):** Level 7 (Master's Level)

### **14. Competency Frameworks**
- The learning outcomes map to the following skills and competencies from the **European Skills, Competences, Qualifications and Occupations (ESCO)** framework:
    - **LO1 Maps to:** `analyse ICT system's performance` (ID: 1189) and `critical thinking` (ID: 15458).
    - **LO2 Maps to:** `develop ICT solutions` (ID: 6223), supported by `machine learning` (ID: 7977), `natural language processing` (ID: 8011), and `computer vision` (ID: 7856).
    - **LO3 Maps to:** `ICT quality assurance methodologies` (ID: 6308) and `apply professional ethics` (ID: 15467).
    - **LO4 Maps to:** `manage ICT project` (ID: 6323) and `software release management` (ID: 10833).
    - **LO5 Maps to:** `communication principles` (ID: 12512) and `explain technical details to non-technical audiences` (ID: 10476).

### **15. Learning Activities**
- The learning experience is delivered through a mix of asynchronous online lectures, interactive coding notebooks (e.g., Jupyter/Colab), synchronous online seminars for discussion, and in-person lab sessions for collaborative project work.

### **16. Workload Theoretical**
- **75 hours:** This includes time spent on lectures, theoretical readings, online quizzes, and self-study to understand core concepts.

### **17. Workload Practical**
- **75 hours:** This includes time spent in seminars and labs, on practical coding exercises, and dedicated work on the programming assignments and the final capstone project.
- **Total Workload:** 150 hours (Equivalent to **6 ECTS**)

### **18. Assessment Methods**
- Learner performance is assessed through a combination of methods, weighted as follows:
    - **Programming Assignments (40%):** Practical assignments to assess the ability to implement complex deep learning solutions (LO2).
    - **Theoretical Quizzes (20%):** Online quizzes to assess the analysis and critique of AI models (LO1).
    - **Capstone Project (40%):** A comprehensive project assessing all learning outcomes. It includes a written report, a codebase, a final presentation, and a containerized model served via a simple API (LO1, LO2, LO3, LO4, LO5).
