### **9. Learning Objectives**
Upon completion of this microcredential, the learner is able to:
1.  **Analyze** the structure of major global education systems.
2.  **Differentiate** between various types of academic and professional credentials.
3.  **Apply** standard methodologies to evaluate foreign credentials against local system standards.
4.  **Utilize** digital tools and databases for credential research and verification.
5.  **Evaluate** the authenticity of credentials using forensic and digital verification techniques.
6.  **Compose** clear and accurate credential evaluation reports for academic and professional purposes.
7.  **Compose** a forensic analysis report detailing findings and evidence related to suspected document fraud.

### **10. Content Description**
The microcredential is composed of three sequential modules, totaling 82.5 hours of learning:
-   **Module 1: Foundations of Global Education Systems (1 ECTS):** Introduction to comparative education, Bologna Process, three-cycle system, and key differences in systems (e.g., US, UK, China, India).
-   **Module 2: The Credential Evaluation Process & Tools (1 ECTS):** Step-by-step evaluation methodology, calculating Grade Point Average (GPA) equivalencies, credit conversion, and using core databases (e.g., WES, ECE, UK ENIC).
-   **Module 3: Advanced Evaluation: Fraud Detection & Digital Verification (1 ECTS):** Common fraud patterns by region, digital document forensics (metadata analysis), introduction to verifiable credentials/blockchain, and handling complex/mixed-system cases.
