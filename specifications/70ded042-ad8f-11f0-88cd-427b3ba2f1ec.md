### 9. Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, learners will be able to:
1.  **Analyze** the theoretical foundations of AI in CSCL, critically evaluating the affordances and constraints of different AI paradigms (e.g., symbolic, connectionist, generative) for supporting group learning processes.
2.  **Design** an AI-driven intervention to address a specific challenge in a collaborative learning environment (e.g., improving group cohesion, facilitating equitable participation, scaffolding complex problem-solving).
3.  **Develop** a functional prototype of an AI-CSCL component by leveraging contemporary generative AI APIs and frameworks, demonstrating technical proficiency.
4.  **Evaluate** the pedagogical, ethical, and social implications of AI-CSCL systems, with a specific focus on systems incorporating generative AI, synthesizing emerging research to formulate evidence-based recommendations for responsible implementation.

### 10. Content Description
The microcredential is structured into three modules:

-   **Module 1: Foundations and Frontiers of AI in CSCL (30 hours)**
    *   **Description:** Establishes the theoretical groundwork, exploring the intersection of learning sciences, collaboration theories, and advanced AI.
    *   **Content:** State-of-the-Art Review: From ITS to AI-CSCL; AI for Group Formation and Moderation; Learning Analytics Dashboards; Natural Language Processing (NLP) for Discourse Analysis; Foundational Models and the role of Generative AI.

-   **Module 2: Designing and Prototyping AI-CSCL Interventions (40 hours)**
    *   **Description:** A core hands-on component where learners design and prototype their capstone project using generative AI.
    *   **Content:** Design Thinking for AI-Enhanced Learning Experiences; **Hands-on Lab: Generative AI for CSCL Prototyping** (Using APIs like OpenAI or Hugging Face to build collaborative agents, discussion summarizers, or Socratic tutors); Data Ethics and Privacy; Prototyping Tools.

-   **Module 3: Evaluation, Ethics, and Future Directions (30 hours)**
    *   **Description:** Focuses on the critical evaluation of AI-CSCL systems, with an emphasis on the ethical challenges posed by generative AI.
    *   **Content:** Frameworks for Evaluating Educational Technologies; Addressing Bias, Fairness, and Transparency in Generative AI for Education; Human-in-the-Loop Design; Capstone Project Showcase and Peer Review.
