## 9. Learning Objectives
Upon successful completion of this microcredential, participants will be able to:
1.  **Analyze** the foundational technological components (IoT, data platforms, AI/ML) that enable smart city initiatives.
2.  **Design** data-driven solutions for common urban challenges, such as traffic management, energy efficiency, and public safety.
3.  **Evaluate** the ethical, societal, and governance implications of smart city deployments.
4.  **Implement** practical smart city applications using relevant tools and programming frameworks in hands-on lab environments.
5.  **Develop** a comprehensive project proposal and prototype for a smart city solution, integrating emerging research and best practices.
6.  **Communicate** effectively about smart city concepts, technologies, and project outcomes to diverse stakeholders.

## 10. Content Description

### Module 1: Foundations of Smart Cities (50 hours / 2 ECTS)
*   **Content**: Introduction to Smart Cities: Concepts, evolution, global initiatives. Internet of Things (IoT) for Urban Environments: Sensor networks, edge computing, communication protocols (LoRaWAN, 5G, NB-IoT). Smart City Data Ecosystems: Data collection, storage, and management. Cyber-Physical Systems in Smart Cities.

### Module 2: Data Analytics and AI for Urban Intelligence (50 hours / 2 ECTS)
*   **Content**: Urban Data Analytics: Descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics. Machine Learning in Smart Cities: Supervised/unsupervised learning, deep learning for image/video analysis (e.g., traffic monitoring). Big Data Platforms: Introduction to Apache Spark/Hadoop for urban data processing. Data Visualization for Urban Planning: Tools and techniques (e.g., Tableau, Power BI, Python libraries).

### Module 3: Smart City Applications, Governance, and Ethics (50 hours / 2 ECTS)
*   **Content**: Key Smart City Verticals: Smart Mobility, Smart Energy Grids, Smart Public Safety, Smart Health, Smart Waste Management. Citizen Engagement and Co-creation in Smart Cities. Ethical AI and Data Privacy in Urban Contexts (e.g., GDPR, facial recognition debates). Smart City Governance, Policy, and Funding Models. Sustainability and Resilience in Smart City Design.
